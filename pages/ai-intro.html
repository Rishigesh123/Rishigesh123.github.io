Artificial Intelligence

The simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, especially computer systems.

DEFINITION

Definition

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a wide-ranging branch of computer science concerned with building smart machines capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence. These tasks include things like recognizing speech, making decisions, translating languages, and identifying patterns.

History of AI

The history of AI is a fascinating journey from ancient myths to modern reality:

  • 1950: Alan Turing proposes the "Turing Test" to determine if a machine can think.
  • 1956: The term "Artificial Intelligence" is coined at the Dartmouth Conference.
  • 1960s: ELIZA, an early natural language processing computer program, is created.
  • 1997: IBM's Deep Blue beats world chess champion Garry Kasparov.
  • 2011: IBM Watson wins Jeopardy!.
  • 2020s: Generative AI models like GPT-4 revolutionize content creation.
