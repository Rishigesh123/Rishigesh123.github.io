Apriori Algorithm

Uncovering hidden relationships in consumer behavior.

ASSOCIATE

The Intuition

The Apriori Algorithm is used for Association Rule Mining. It identifies frequent itemsets in a database and generates rules to predict the occurrence of an item based on the presence of others.

The classic example is "Market Basket Analysis": If a customer buys bread, they are very likely to buy butter as well. Apriori helps stores arrange products to boost sales.

The Key Metrics

Apriori relies on three major indicators to evaluate the strength of a relationship:

  • Support: How popular the itemset is (e.g., how many transactions contain both Bread and Butter).
  • Confidence: How likely B is purchased when A is purchased (e.g., likelihood of buying Butter given Bread is in the cart).
  • Lift: How much more likely B is bought with A compared to if B was bought randomly.

How It Works

  1. Scan: Browse the transaction database to find frequent single items.
  2. Prune: Discard items that appear less than a "Minimum Support" threshold.
  3. Join: Combine the remaining items to form pairs (2-itemsets).
  4. Repeat: Continue forming 3-itemsets, 4-itemsets, etc., until no more frequent itemsets can be found.
  5. Rule Generation: Create rules (If A, then B) that meet "Minimum Confidence."
