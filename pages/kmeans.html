K-Means Clustering

Mastering the art of grouping data points based on similarity.

CLUSTERS

The Intuition

K-Means is an unsupervised learning algorithm that groups data points into K clusters. It works by finding the central point (centroid) for each cluster and assigning every data point to its nearest centroid.

Imagine sorting a box of mixed buttons by color. You pick a few "example" buttons (centroids) and put every other button in the pile it looks most like.

How It Works

  1. Choose K: Decide how many clusters you want (e.g., K=3).
  2. Initialize: Randomly place K centroids in the data space.
  3. Assign: Each data point is linked to its closest centroid.
  4. Update: Move each centroid to the mean (center) of its assigned points.
  5. Repeat: Continue until centroids stop moving.

Mathematical Objective

The algorithm aims to minimize the Within-Cluster Sum of Squares (WCSS), also known as Inertia.

WCSS = Σ Σ || xᵢ - μⱼ ||²

Where μⱼ is the centroid of cluster j and xᵢ is a data point in that cluster.

The "Elbow" Method

How do we find the perfect K? We plot WCSS against different values of K. The point where the graph looks like an "elbow" is usually the optimal number of clusters.

ALGORITHM TYPE

Unsupervised (Clustering)

COMPLEXITY

O(n * k * i * d)

n: nodes, k: clusters, i: iterations, d: features

STRENGTHS

  • Simple & Fast
  • Scalable
  • Easy to Interpret

WEAKNESSES

  • Sensitive to Outliers
  • Needs K in advance
  • Struggles with non-spherical shapes
